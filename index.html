Lyndon Koens
Macquaire University

Dynamics of microsystems

Colloids, bacteria and micromachines all display complex dynamical behaviour. This is due to the dynamics of an object at this scale critically depends on its physical interactions and shape. In my work I develop models to understand and model these emergent phenomena.

Methods and Techniques

In my work I have dealt with viscous flows, Brownian motion, surface tension, electromagnetism and quantum mechanics. Typically in these explorations I use asymptotic and numerical techniques to develop simple and accurate models for microscopic dynamic systems. In doing so I have regularised singular integrals, inverted integral equations and evaluated stochastic differential equations.
